Pursuing CCNA & Hands-On Labs

I am committed to building a strong foundation in networking and cybersecurity, both through formal certification and hands-on practice. I am currently studying for the CCNA certification through Coursera and YouTube resources, covering core topics like IP addressing, routing protocols, switching, and network security fundamentals.

However, I recognize that the CCNA is largely a theoretical exam - passing it doesn't necessarily mean you can configure a real network. To bridge that gap, I've taken the practical knowledge from my CCNA studies and applied it hands-on in Cisco Packet Tracer, building and troubleshooting simulated networks. This includes configuring VLANs and inter-VLAN routing, setting up DNS and DHCP servers, implementing OSPF routing, configuring ACLs for traffic filtering, and applying NAT for address translation.

I've also completed the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate on Coursera, and built a home lab using VirtualBox, where I deployed Active Directory, configured Windows Server 2022, and set up Splunk SIEM with Sysmon for endpoint monitoring - simulating real attacks to understand both offensive techniques and defensive detection.

  • CCNA Certification (in progress)
  • Expanding my home lab with firewall configuration (pfSense) and VPN setup
